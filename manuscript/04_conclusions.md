## 5. Conclusions

This study delivered the first integrated predict-optimise-simulate pipeline for DTP1-to-DTP3 dropout reduction in Nigeria using the 2024 NDHS. Cascade-specific XGBoost classifiers discriminated well at both dose transitions after isotonic recalibration (T1 AUROC 0.910; T2 AUROC 0.943), and an offline RL policy trained on behaviour-inferred DHS trajectories increased simulated DTP3 coverage by 1.07 percentage points over the status quo. The central cost-effectiveness finding is that the simpler risk-targeted strategy — universal SMS reminders plus CHW escalation for the top-decile dropout-risk children — dominated the RL-optimised scenario at ₦50,000/pp willingness-to-pay (ICER ₦301/pp versus ₦699/pp; cost-effectiveness probability 56.7% versus 0%). The RL policy's equity advantage — a ~10% relative narrowing of the wealth-quintile DTP3 completion gap — may justify deployment at higher willingness-to-pay thresholds. For NPHCDA and immunisation programme managers, cascade-specific risk scoring combined with cost-effectiveness microsimulation and an open dashboard provides a deployable triage layer for DTP cascade completion; the immediate cost-effective action at current budget parameters is risk-stratified outreach — broad SMS with CHW escalation for the highest-risk children.
